Autonomous Flower Detection System

2025 MSc Dissertation

Overview

For my MSc dissertation in Biorobotics at the University of Bristol, I built a computer vision pipeline capable of detecting and tracking flowers in real time — designed for deployment in precision agriculture environments where automated pollination robots need to navigate complex, dynamic plant canopies.

The core challenge was reliability under real-world conditions: variable lighting, occlusion from leaves, and flowers at unpredictable angles. I used transfer learning on top of YOLOv8, fine-tuned on a custom annotated dataset captured across different growth stages and lighting conditions.

What I built

  • Real-time YOLOv8-based detection pipeline running on embedded hardware
  • Robust preprocessing to normalise lighting and handle occlusion
  • Depth estimation integration for 3D localisation of flower positions
  • Robotic control interface for passing coordinates to a manipulator arm
  • Custom training dataset with bounding-box annotations across multiple plant species

Tech stack

Impact

The system demonstrated consistent detection accuracy under challenging field conditions, with a pipeline capable of supporting fully autonomous pollination robots. The research contributes to solving one of agriculture's most pressing problems — the global decline in natural pollinators — through precision, scalable automation.
